# Installation

FlowMail is a desktop app for macOS, available for Apple Silicon Macs (M1, M2, M3, and M4 chips).

## System requirements

- **macOS** (Sonoma 14.0 or later recommended)
- **Apple Silicon** (M1, M2, M3, or M4) — Intel Macs are not supported

## Download

Download the latest version of FlowMail from the [GitHub releases page](https://github.com/ljchang/flowmail-site/releases/latest).

Look for the `.dmg` file in the release assets.

## Install

1. Open the downloaded `.dmg` file.
2. Drag the FlowMail app icon into the **Applications** folder.
3. Eject the DMG from Finder.

## First launch

When you open FlowMail for the first time, macOS may show a security warning. This happens because FlowMail is signed by an independent developer rather than distributed through the Mac App Store.

To open the app:

1. **Right-click** (or Control-click) on FlowMail in your Applications folder.
2. Select **Open** from the context menu.
3. In the dialog that appears, click **Open** to confirm.

You only need to do this once. After the first launch, FlowMail will open normally like any other app.

## Initial setup

On first launch, FlowMail will automatically set up its local encrypted database. This only takes a moment. Once complete, you'll be guided through connecting your email accounts and configuring AI features.

All your data is stored locally on your Mac — nothing is sent to external servers beyond what's needed to fetch your email and communicate with your chosen AI provider.
