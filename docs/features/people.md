# People & Relationships

Email is fundamentally about people. FlowMail builds a living contact directory from your email interactions, so the AI always knows who you're talking to and how you communicate with them.

## Automatic Contact Discovery

FlowMail extracts contact information from your email headers and message content. You don't need to manually add contacts or maintain an address book — your directory builds itself as you use email.

## Relationship Context

Each contact tracks meaningful details about your working relationship:

- **Relationship type** — Colleague, manager, client, vendor, friend, or any custom label
- **Communication tone** — Formal, casual, or somewhere in between
- **Interaction frequency** — How often you exchange emails, helping the AI understand the importance of the relationship

## The People View

Browse your contacts with a clear view of recent interaction history. See at a glance who you've been in touch with, when your last exchange was, and what it was about. This makes it easy to follow up with people you haven't heard from in a while.

## Delegation

When something is better handled by someone else, delegate it directly from FlowMail. Forward items to specific people with the relevant context attached — they get what they need, and the item moves to your "Waiting" queue so you remember to follow up.

## Smarter Drafting

Contact context is one of the most powerful inputs to [AI Drafting](/docs/features/ai-drafting). When FlowMail drafts a response, it adjusts tone and formality based on your relationship with the recipient:

- A reply to your CEO will be polished and professional
- A reply to a close teammate will be direct and casual
- A reply to a new client will strike the right balance of warmth and professionalism

You don't have to think about tone-switching — FlowMail handles it automatically based on the relationship context it has built over time.

## People as Context

People can be used as contexts when composing emails — helping the AI generate better drafts by drawing on your relationship history. They also help FlowMail figure out who an email should be sent to. When you describe what you need ("email my manager about the deadline" or "check in with the design team"), FlowMail uses your contact graph to identify the right recipients.
